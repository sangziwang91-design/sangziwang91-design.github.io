# Public Language Rewrite Plan v0.1

This document defines the public homepage language direction.

## Target public identity

**AI Behavioral Evaluation and Runtime Safety Audit**

## Method label

**Black-Box Behavioral Cartography**

## Homepage opening line

I study how AI systems behave across structured interaction conditions: how observable responses stabilize, drift, over-complete, imitate confidence, lose epistemic grounding, become measurement-invalid, or recover after interruption.

## Public homepage structure

1. Research focus
2. Public records
3. External language layer
4. Evidence status
5. Claim ceiling
6. Disclosure boundary
7. Next research gate
8. Contact

## Public claim ceiling

The homepage should claim:

- restricted-access behavioral observability;
- black-box response-trajectory mapping;
- public-safe evidence records;
- sandbox-level runtime validation;
- preparation for reproducible task subsets.

The homepage should not claim:

- solved alignment;
- production deployment;
- model-internal interpretability;
- control over intrinsic model distributions;
- validated benchmark or standard status;
- peer-reviewed acceptance unless explicitly true.

## Risk control

- Do not expose private implementation details.
- Do not list internal operational components in a way that invites reverse engineering.
- Do not present internal maturity labels as external standards.
- Do not make acceptance, funding, or institutional endorsement claims before they exist.
- Keep DOI records as public timestamped artifacts, not peer-review substitutes.

## Implementation note

The homepage can be rewritten in small commits after this plan is reviewed.
